  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究有限度量偏序集上模的最小射影分解的稳定性问题，旨在建立一个统一的框架，将经典的持久同调稳定性理论推广到多参数和更一般的代数拓扑情境。

Method: 1. 在模侧引入Galois传输距离，通过因子化两个模通过共同的'顶点'偏序集来衡量指标偏序集中的最大位移；2. 在同调侧定义最小射影分解之间的瓶颈距离，通过逐级匹配不可分解射影来进行度量；3. 主要定理证明分解层次的瓶颈距离总被Galois传输距离控制。

Result: 建立了Galois传输距离与最小射影分解瓶颈距离之间的稳定性不等式，在单参数情境下恢复经典的瓶颈稳定性定理，在多参数情境下扩展到由最小射影分解产生的有符号图示。

Abstract: We develop a stability theory for minimal projective resolutions of $\mathbf{P}$-modules, where $\mathbf{P}$ is a finite metric poset. On the module side, we introduce the \emph{Galois transport distance}, defined by factoring two modules through a common ``apex'' poset via pairs of Galois insertions and measuring the maximal displacement in the index poset. This construction generalizes the interleaving distance in both the classical one-parameter and multiparameter settings, and yields an extended metric on isomorphism classes of $\mathbf{P}$-modules.

Our main theorem shows that this resolution-level bottleneck distance is always bounded above by the Galois transport distance, providing a metric stability result formulated entirely at the level of modules and their minimal projective resolutions.
  We then treat persistence as an application. Passing to the interval poset and a kernel construction, we interpret persistence diagrams as minimal projective resolutions of kernel modules and obtain a corresponding stability inequality. In the one-parameter case this recovers classical bottleneck stability, while in the multiparameter setting it extends naturally to signed diagrams arising from minimal projective resolutions. Via a general relationship between minimal projective resolutions and Möbius inversion, these results can be interpreted as a stability theorem for Möbius homology, while remaining entirely phrased in the language of projective resolutions.

Method: 1) 明确描述最大刚性集上的操作，这个操作包含了silting变异的对偶。2) 建立挠对格中的wide区间与Ziegler谱中闭刚性集之间的双射。3) 证明挠对格的Hasse箭图与几乎完备闭刚性集（即恰好以两种方式补全为最大刚性集的集合）的对应关系。4) 利用Ziegler谱的拓扑确定可变异点

Our results are most interesting in the case when $A$ is a finite dimensional algebra. In fact, we generalise results by Adachi, Iyama and Reiten, with an important difference: not every point in a maximal rigid set is mutable. We use the topology on the Ziegler spectrum to determine the mutable points. In the last section of the paper we illustrate our results by the example of a finite dimensional algebra arising from a triangulation of an annulus.

Method: 通过显式计算发现现象，基于全局现象提出局部猜想，描述固定权重、变化L不变量、具有相反Atkin-Lehner符号的半稳定表示之间的同余关系，并提供理论证据支持。

This is a new phenomenon that is not explained by the known results on the $p$-adic variation of eigenforms. Inspired by the global picture, we formulate a local conjecture describing congruences between semistable representations of fixed weight, varying $\mathcal L$-invariant, and opposite Atkin--Lehner signs. We give some theoretical evidence towards our conjectures.
