Abstract: Let $\mathbb F$ be an algebraically closed field of characteristic $0$. Let $k\geq 2$ be an integer, and let $n\in \mathbb F[x]\setminus\{0\}$. For $k\ge18$, we prove that every such tuple satisfies $|A|\le6$, except for the necessary exceptional family in which $n=s^2$ is a $k$-th power and $A\subset s\mathbb{F}$. This bound is absolute: it is independent of both $n$ and $\operatorname{deg} n$. Our proof develops a new method for studying polynomial Diophantine tuples, combining a determinant criterion, generalizations of the Mason--Stothers theorem, and the Combinatorial Nullstellensatz. We also record a conditional analogue for generalized Diophantine tuples over the integers.

Abstract: We establish sharp lower bounds for the Dirichlet character moments $\frac{1}{r-1} \sum_{χ\; \text{mod} \; r} |\sum_{n \leq x} χ(n)|^{2q}$, where $r$ is a large prime, $1 \leq x \leq r^{0.499}$, and $0 \leq q \leq 1$ is real. ### [24] [On a conjecture of Andrews and almost alternating sign patterns](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01210)

Main category: math.NT

TL;DR: 论文证明了Andrews在Ramanujan丢失笔记本中发现的某些q-级数的符号现象，表明v2(q)、v3(q)、v4(q)的系数仅在密度为零的例外点上交替符号，并建立了q-超几何级数的无限族也具有相同的符号行为。

Motivation: Andrews在Ramanujan丢失笔记本中观察到某些q-级数的系数符号规律，即v2(q)、v3(q)、v4(q)的系数呈现交替符号的趋势，作者旨在证明这一现象并理解其背后的数学原理。

Method: 采用adapted circle method（改良圆法），灵感来自Folsom-Males-Rolen-Storzer关于v1(q)的工作，通过渐近分析研究这些q-级数在单位根附近的振荡行为，从而揭示指数增长与振荡之间的相互作用如何产生交替符号因子。

Result: 证明了v2(q)、v3(q)、v4(q)的系数在除密度为零的稀疏例外点外均交替为负，建立了相同符号行为适用于显式无穷族q-超几何级数，并表明这源于这些q-级数在单位根附近的振荡渐近式。此外还发现另一个家族似乎也表现出类似的符号规律。

Conclusion: 这种符号现象是普遍存在的，可能指向更深的底层理论。研究者引入了一个额外的系数似乎表现出类似符号规律的家族，表明这一现象在q-超几何级数中广泛存在。

Abstract: In this paper, we prove a sign phenomenon first observed by Andrews for certain $q$-series from Ramanujan's Lost Notebook. For three of the series considered by Andrews, namely $v_2(q)$, $v_3(q)$, and $v_4(q)$, we show that the coefficients are alternating in sign, with only a density-zero set of exceptions. Our approach yields precise asymptotic formulas for the coefficients via an adapted circle method, inspired by the work of Folsom-Males-Rolen-Storzer on the $q$-series $v_1(q)$, revealing an interplay between exponential growth and oscillatory behaviour. This interaction produces a dominant alternating sign factor, which governs the sign regularity observed numerically by Andrews. More broadly, we establish the same sign behaviour for explicit infinite families of $q$-hypergeometric series encompassing these examples, and show that it arises systematically from oscillatory asymptotics of these $q$-series near roots of unity. We introduce an additional family whose coefficients appear to exhibit similar sign regularity, suggesting that this phenomenon is widespread and may point towards a deeper underlying theory.
