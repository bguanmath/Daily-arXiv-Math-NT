### [2] [Exceptions to the Erd\H os--Straus--Schinzel conjecture](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.16817)
*Carl Pomerance,Andreas Weingartner*

Main category: math.NT

TL;DR: 对Erdős–Straus和Sierpiński等人关于分数分解为三个单位分数之和的猜想进行的研究，表明当m较大时，存在n使得m/n不能表示为三个单位分数之和，并给出了具体下界。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 验证Erdős-Straus以及推广到m/n的分数分解猜想，特别是确定是否存在无穷多个n使得m/n不能表示为三个单位分数之和。

Method: 推广Elsholtz和Tao的工作，数值计算和证明了一个明确下界：存在素数p在(m^2,2m^2)区间内，使得m/p不能表示为三个单位分数之和。

Result: 证明当m ≥ 6.52×10⁹时，存在这样的素数p，从而表明n的下界至少为exp(m^{1/3+o(1)})；小规模数值计算支持m≥19即存在反例。

Conclusion: m/n分解为三个单位分数并非对所有足够大的n成立，存在大整数n使得分解失败；该结论推广到更多单位分数的情形。

Abstract: A famous conjecture of Erd\H os and Straus is that for every integer $n\ge2$, $4/n$ can be represented as $1/x+1/y+1/z$, where $x,y,z$ are positive integers. This conjecture was generalized to $5/n$ by Sierpiński, and then Schinzel conjectured that for every integer $m\ge4$ there is a bound $n_m$ such that the fraction $m/n$ is the sum of 3 unit fractions for all integers $n\ge n_m$. Leveraging and generalizing work of Elsholtz and Tao, we show that if $n_m$ exists it must be at least $\exp(m^{1/3+o(1)})$; that is, there are numbers $n$ this large for which $m/n$ is not the sum of 3 unit fractions. We prove a weaker, but numerically explicit version of this theorem, showing that for $m\ge 6.52\times10^9$ there is a prime $p\in(m^2,2m^2)$ with $m/p$ not the sum of 3 unit fractions, and report on some extensive numerical calculations that support this assertion with the much smaller bound $m\ge19$. In addition we generalize a result of Vaughan to show that for each $m$, most $n$'s have $m/n$ representable, and we prove a result generalizing the problem to the sum of $j$ unit fractions.
